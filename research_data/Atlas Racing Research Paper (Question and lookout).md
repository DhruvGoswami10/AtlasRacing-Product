# Atlas Racing Research Paper

## Your Research Question

> "Can Large Language Models effectively serve as AI Race Engineers when provided with structured real-time telemetry context?"
> 

### Sub-questions:

1. How accurately can an LLM provide contextually appropriate strategic advice during races?
2. Does LLM race engineering assistance improve pit stop timing decisions?
3. What is the effect on driver cognitive load and race performance?
4. How do drivers perceive and trust LLM-generated racing advice?

## How to Frame This in Your Paper

**Introduction paragraph:**

> "While recent work has applied machine learning to race strategy optimization (Thomas et al., 2025; Heilmeier et al., 2020) and tire degradation prediction (Cappello & Hoegh, 2025; Todd et al., 2025), these approaches focus on automated decision systems without natural language interfaces. Adjacent research on AI coaching (Kaufman et al., 2024) has explored driving skill improvement but not real-time strategic advice. Commercial products such as CrewChief provide rule-based alerts, but lack the contextual reasoning of large language models. To our knowledge, no prior work has investigated the effectiveness of LLMs as race engineers when provided with structured telemetry context. This paper addresses that gap."
>

The core of your research question is really about whether an LLM, in a general sense, can serve as a race engineer when it’s given that structured, real-time telemetry. It’s not about which specific model you use—whether it’s GPT, Claude, Grok, or anything else. Those are just different tools you could test.

Your research is more about the concept: can a large language model, in general, take this kind of data and give useful, context-aware strategy advice? And if the answer is yes, then it shows that LLMs have potential in that role regardless of which exact model you’re using.
